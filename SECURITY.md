# Security Policy

At PDFMinty, the security and privacy of our users are extremely important. Since our application focuses on absolute privacy by processing sensitive PDF documents directly inside the browser sandbox, any potential security vulnerability is treated with the highest priority.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability in this project, we kindly ask you to report it to us privately. Please do not create public GitHub issues or discuss potential exploits in public forums before we have had a chance to address the problem.

Please report security issues and vulnerabilities by emailing us directly:

**Email:** security@pdfminty.com

### What to Include in Your Report

When submitting a vulnerability report, please provide detailed information to help us reproduce and understand the issue:

1. **Description:** A detailed description of the vulnerability.
2. **Steps to Reproduce:** A clear step-by-step guide to reproducing the issue.
3. **Impact:** Your assessment of the potential impact (e.g., exposing local memory, XSS in the client-side tool workspace, bypass of backend rate limits).
4. **Environment:** The browser, operating system, and any other relevant configurations you used when discovering the issue.
5. **Mitigation:** If you have suggestions for fixing the issue or a proof-of-concept patch, please include them.

## Handling Process

Our team will investigate your report promptly. Here is what you can expect:
- **Acknowledgement:** We aim to acknowledge receipt of your report within 48 hours.
- **Triage & Investigation:** We will review the vulnerability, assess its severity, and plan a patch.
- **Resolution:** After a fix is developed and deployed, we will inform you of the resolution and coordinate with you regarding public disclosure.

We are committed to maintaining a strict no-user-data-upload policy and greatly appreciate the community's help in maintaining this secure environment.
